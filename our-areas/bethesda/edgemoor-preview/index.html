Edgemoor, Bethesda MD: Homes, Schools & Neighborhood Guide

An established luxury neighborhood of large homes, mature trees, and walkable streets — just northwest of downtown Bethesda.

View Homes for Sale Get Home Value
Overview

One of Bethesda’s most established luxury neighborhoods

Edgemoor is one of Bethesda’s oldest and most established luxury residential neighborhoods — a close-in enclave of large homes, mature tree canopy, and quiet, walkable streets just northwest of downtown Bethesda. Within a few blocks of Bethesda Row, the Bethesda Metro, and Arlington Road, Edgemoor pairs real architectural character and generous lots with a location most Bethesda neighborhoods simply can’t match.

Homes here turn over slowly, hold their value, and are often held within families for years. That low turnover is exactly what makes Edgemoor both highly desirable and genuinely difficult to buy into — and why a focused, local strategy matters more here than almost anywhere else in Bethesda. Start with the Bethesda neighborhood guide for the wider market, or read on for everything specific to Edgemoor.

Edgemoor at a Glance

  • Homes: Traditional luxury single-family — Colonials, Tudors, Georgians & custom estates

  • Character: Mature trees, large lots, low turnover, walkable to downtown

Location

Where Is Edgemoor in Bethesda?

Edgemoor sits immediately northwest of downtown Bethesda, generally between Arlington Road and Old Georgetown Road and within easy walking distance of Bethesda Row, Wisconsin Avenue, and the Bethesda Metro station on the Red Line. The Capital Crescent Trail and the shops and restaurants of downtown Bethesda are minutes away, and the Chevy Chase Club area lies just to the southeast.

What sets Edgemoor apart is how close-in it is for a neighborhood of this caliber of home. Residents can walk to dining, the farmers market, the Metro, and the trail — a level of everyday convenience that’s rare for an area defined by large single-family houses on established lots. Identity

What Is Edgemoor Known For?

Edgemoor’s reputation rests on a few specific things. First, prestige and longevity: it is one of Bethesda’s original luxury neighborhoods, and its addresses have held their standing for generations. Second, mature tree canopy and established streetscapes — the landscaping here has had decades to grow in, giving the streets a settled, leafy character that newer subdivisions can’t replicate.

Third, proximity to downtown Bethesda. Few neighborhoods offer this combination of large homes and a truly walkable downtown with a Metro station. And fourth, low turnover: owners tend to stay for the long term, which keeps inventory scarce and competition meaningful when a home does come up. Together, these traits make Edgemoor feel less like a subdivision and more like a long-established, close-in address.

Housing

Homes in Edgemoor

Edgemoor is overwhelmingly a neighborhood of single-family detached luxury homes. You’ll find classic Colonials, Tudors, and Georgians alongside thoughtfully renovated older houses and newer custom estates built to take advantage of the larger, more mature lots. Compared with many close-in Bethesda pockets, lot sizes and setbacks here tend to be more generous, and the tree cover more established.

Price positioning is firmly in Bethesda’s upper tier, and inventory is consistently low — a function of the neighborhood’s desirability and its long-term owners. Architecture

Architecture in Edgemoor

Architecturally, Edgemoor is defined by traditional luxury — brick and stucco Colonials, Tudor revivals, Georgians, and custom estates, many dating to the neighborhood’s early decades and others rebuilt or extensively renovated for modern living. The prevailing language is formal, established, and rooted in classic Mid-Atlantic residential design.

Within that traditional context, Edgemoor also holds rare examples of serious contemporary architecture. The most notable is the David Jameson–designed residence at 7118 Glenbrook Road. David Jameson, FAIA, is a Washington, DC–based architect whose residential work has earned national recognition, including a 2012 AIA National Honor Award. The Glenbrook Road house — documented by the USModernist architectural archive and widely published — is a glass-and-stone contemporary composition that stands as a striking exception within a neighborhood otherwise known for its Colonials and Tudors. You can see more of the architect’s work at David Jameson Architect.

Community

The Edgemoor Club

No discussion of Edgemoor is complete without The Edgemoor Club, the private tennis and swim club that has been woven into the neighborhood’s identity since 1920. Located on Exeter Road, the member-owned club is known for its clay tennis courts and a heated competitive pool, and it has long functioned as a social anchor — hosting tennis and swim teams, lessons and clinics, and a calendar of member events.

For many families, the club is part of what makes Edgemoor feel like a community rather than just a collection of fine houses. Access is limited to members and their guests; details and membership information are available on the club’s official website. (Please verify current offerings directly with the club.)

Schools

Schools Serving Edgemoor

Most homes in Edgemoor are served by Montgomery County Public Schools (MCPS), with typical assignments to Bethesda Elementary School, Westland Middle School, and Bethesda-Chevy Chase High School. These are among the more sought-after assignments in the county, which adds to the neighborhood’s appeal for families.

Important: School assignments can vary within a neighborhood and change over time. Always confirm the exact assignment for a specific address directly with Montgomery County Public Schools before making any decision based on schools.

Commute

Commuting From Edgemoor

Edgemoor’s location is one of its biggest practical advantages. The Bethesda Metro station (Red Line) is within walking distance for much of the neighborhood, offering a direct ride into downtown Washington, DC and out to Montgomery County job centers. Wisconsin Avenue and Old Georgetown Road provide quick north-south routes, while Connecticut Avenue and the surrounding arterials connect to Chevy Chase and upper Northwest DC.

Drivers reach the Capital Beltway (I-495) in minutes, and downtown DC, Friendship Heights, and the medical and research corridors along Rockville Pike are all easily accessible. For day-to-day life, much of what residents need is simply a walk away in downtown Bethesda — which is exactly why the neighborhood commands the prices it does.

For Buyers

Buying a Home in Edgemoor

Buying in Edgemoor rewards preparation. Because inventory is limited and good homes attract attention, serious buyers should be fully pre-approved, financially decisive, and ready to act when the right property appears. Waiting for a large selection to choose from rarely works here; the neighborhood simply doesn’t produce that kind of volume.

It also pays to look beyond the public listings. Some of the best opportunities in Edgemoor are off-market or “coming soon,” surfacing through local relationships before they ever hit the open market. Working with an agent who knows the neighborhood — and who hears about homes early — can be the difference between buying in Edgemoor and watching from the sidelines. New to the process? For Sellers

Selling a Home in Edgemoor

Selling in Edgemoor is less about volume and more about positioning to the right buyer. Edgemoor buyers are paying for a specific combination of qualities, and a successful sale highlights them clearly: architectural quality and character, condition and updates, the lot and its setting, location and walkability to downtown Bethesda, and — where it applies — the provenance or design pedigree of the home.

Because these homes sit at the top of the market, presentation, pricing strategy, and storytelling carry real weight. The goal is to make the home’s strengths unmistakable to a discerning, well-qualified audience. Quick Answers

Edgemoor FAQ

Where is Edgemoor in Bethesda? +

Edgemoor sits immediately northwest of downtown Bethesda, Maryland, in ZIP code 20814 — generally between Arlington Road and Old Georgetown Road and within walking distance of Bethesda Row, Wisconsin Avenue, and the Bethesda Metro station on the Red Line. Its close-in location is a defining feature: residents can walk to downtown dining, shopping, the farmers market, and the Capital Crescent Trail.

What types of homes are in Edgemoor? +

Edgemoor is predominantly single-family detached luxury homes — traditional Colonials, Tudors, and Georgians alongside renovated older houses and newer custom estates. Lots tend to be larger and more mature than in many close-in Bethesda neighborhoods, and the streets are known for their tree canopy and established landscaping.

Is Edgemoor considered a luxury neighborhood? +

Yes. Edgemoor is one of Bethesda’s most established and prestigious residential neighborhoods, with high price positioning, large homes on generous lots, and consistently low turnover. Its combination of architectural character and walkable proximity to downtown Bethesda keeps demand strong and inventory tight.

What is The Edgemoor Club? +

The Edgemoor Club is a private, member-owned tennis and swim club that has been part of the neighborhood since 1920. Located on Exeter Road, it offers clay tennis courts and a heated competitive pool and has long served as a social hub for members and their guests. Access is limited to members and their guests.

Are there modern homes in Edgemoor? +

Edgemoor is best known for traditional luxury architecture, but it does contain rare examples of serious contemporary design. The most notable is the David Jameson–designed residence at 7118 Glenbrook Road, a published contemporary house by an award-winning Washington, DC architect, set within a neighborhood otherwise defined by Colonials, Tudors, and Georgians.

How close is Edgemoor to downtown Bethesda? +

Very close. Most of Edgemoor is within roughly a half-mile of Bethesda Row and the Bethesda Metro station, making it one of the few neighborhoods where buyers can have large single-family homes and a genuinely walkable downtown lifestyle. The Red Line offers a direct ride into Washington, DC.

How often do homes come up for sale in Edgemoor? +

Infrequently. Edgemoor has notably low turnover — many owners stay for decades — so the number of homes for sale at any given time is small. Because inventory is limited and competition can be strong, serious buyers often need to move quickly and stay alert to off-market and coming-soon opportunities.
